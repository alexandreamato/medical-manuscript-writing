# Ethics and Research Integrity (Medical Research)

These items must be addressed in every medical manuscript. Reviewers and editors check them early.

## 1. Ethics Approval

1. State the name of the IRB / ethics committee that approved the study and the protocol number.
2. State the date of approval, or the date range if multiple approvals (e.g., across centers).
3. State that the study was conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki and applicable national regulations.
4. For animal studies, state the institutional animal care committee approval and ARRIVE compliance.

Sentence skeleton:

1. `The study was approved by the [IRB / ethics committee name] (protocol [number]) on [date] and was conducted in accordance with the Declaration of Helsinki.`

## 2. Informed Consent

1. State that written informed consent was obtained from all participants (or legal guardians for minors / surrogates for adults unable to consent).
2. If consent was waived, state the specific basis (registry-based study, deceased participants, deidentified data, etc.).
3. For case reports and case series, written informed consent for publication (and, for images, consent for publication of images) is typically required.

## 3. Trial / Review Registration

1. **Trials** must be registered prospectively in a public registry. Most accepted registries: ClinicalTrials.gov (NCT), ISRCTN, EU Clinical Trials Register (EudraCT), JPRN, ANZCTR, ChiCTR, REBEC, the Brazilian Registry of Clinical Trials.
2. State the registry name and identifier in the abstract and Methods.
3. **Systematic reviews** should be registered in PROSPERO before data extraction begins.
4. For reviews of diagnostic accuracy, animal studies, or other special types: use the appropriate prospective registry (e.g., PROSPERO for animal preclinical reviews, INPLASY for systematic reviews of any type).
5. ICMJE-member journals will not publish unregistered trials.

## 4. Authorship (ICMJE Criteria)

All four criteria must be met for authorship:

1. Substantial contributions to conception or design; or acquisition, analysis, or interpretation.
2. Drafting or critical revision of the manuscript for important intellectual content.
3. Final approval of the version to be published.
4. Agreement to be accountable for all aspects of the work.

Contributors who do not meet all four criteria should be acknowledged, not listed as authors.

Provide a CRediT-style contribution statement listing each author's role: Conceptualization, Methodology, Software, Validation, Formal analysis, Investigation, Resources, Data curation, Writing – original draft, Writing – review and editing, Visualization, Supervision, Project administration, Funding acquisition.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

1. Each author discloses financial and non-financial conflicts: employment, consultancy, stock ownership, honoraria, expert testimony, patents, grants, royalties.
2. Use the ICMJE Disclosure Form (https://www.icmje.org/disclosure-of-interest/).
3. Disclose conflicts even if the author judges them irrelevant; the editor decides relevance.
4. State the funder's role in design, conduct, analysis, interpretation, and decision to submit. If the funder had no role, say so explicitly.

## 6. Data and Code Sharing

1. State whether de-identified individual participant data, the analysis code, the protocol, the statistical analysis plan, and the consent form will be shared.
2. State the conditions, the time horizon, and the platform (institutional repository, journal repository, controlled-access platform).
3. ICMJE requires a data sharing statement for clinical trials.

Sentence skeletons:

1. `De-identified individual participant data and the statistical analysis plan will be available upon reasonable request from the corresponding author starting [date], for [purpose].`
2. `Analysis code is available at [GitHub / Zenodo URL with DOI].`

## 7. AI Disclosure

Most major journals (NEJM, Nature, Lancet, JAMA, BMJ, Cell, Science) now require disclosure of AI tool use in writing, analysis, or image generation. Standard rules:

1. AI tools (including large language models) are not authors.
2. Disclose any substantive use of AI in the Methods or Acknowledgments. Specify the tool, the version, and what it was used for.
3. The authors take full responsibility for all content, including any AI-assisted text or analysis.
4. AI-generated figures and AI-altered medical images are typically prohibited unless explicitly methodological (e.g., a study of the AI itself).

Sentence skeleton:

1. `We used [tool, version] to [edit language / draft sections / assist with literature search / assist with code]. The authors verified all output and take full responsibility for the final manuscript.`

## 8. Reporting Integrity

1. **No fabricated data, no fabricated references, no image manipulation.** These are research misconduct and grounds for retraction.
2. Report all prespecified outcomes — including null and adverse findings.
3. Distinguish prespecified analyses from post-hoc / exploratory analyses.
4. Plagiarism, including self-plagiarism, must be avoided. Run a similarity check before submission.
5. Disclose prior versions: preprints, conference abstracts, theses. State the relationship between this manuscript and any prior version.
6. Image integrity: keep raw image files; do not adjust beyond linear contrast/brightness applied to the entire image; do not splice lanes from different gels into a single image.
7. Statistical integrity: provide enough detail (model, software, code) for an independent reader to reproduce the analysis.

## 9. Patient Privacy

1. Do not include identifiable patient information unless necessary and consented.
2. Remove direct identifiers and quasi-identifiers from data and supplementary tables.
3. For images of patients, obtain explicit written consent for publication or use anonymization techniques (cropping, masking, deidentification).
4. For genomic data, comply with controlled-access requirements (dbGaP, EGA).

## 10. Pre-Submission Integrity Checklist

1. Ethics approval reported with committee name and protocol number.
2. Consent reported, or waiver justified.
3. Registration number reported (trials and systematic reviews).
4. Authorship list meets ICMJE criteria; contributions stated.
5. Conflicts of interest fully disclosed.
6. Funder role stated.
7. Data and code sharing statement present.
8. AI disclosure statement present.
9. Every reference is real and verifiable; citations in order of first appearance.
10. No fabricated images; raw data archived.
11. Reporting checklist (CONSORT/STROBE/PRISMA/STARD) submitted.
12. Patient privacy preserved across the manuscript and supplementary files.
