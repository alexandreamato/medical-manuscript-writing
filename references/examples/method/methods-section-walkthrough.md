# Methods Section Annotated Walk-Through

This file walks through a hypothetical Methods section for an RCT of inhaled budesonide for the prevention of progression of mild COVID-19 and labels each paragraph with the three-element role.

## Block-by-Block Mapping

### 2.1 Trial design

1. **Rationale (design):** "We conducted a multicenter, open-label, randomized clinical trial because of the urgency of evaluating widely available, low-cost interventions during the pandemic; the open-label design was selected after assessing that the primary outcome could be ascertained without unblinded interpretation."
2. **Procedure:** "Eligible adults were randomly assigned 1:1 to inhaled budesonide 800 mcg twice daily for 14 days plus usual care, or to usual care alone."
3. **Safeguard:** "Outcome ascertainment was performed by assessors blinded to assignment, and the primary outcome required objective adjudication."

### 2.2 Participants

1. **Rationale (eligibility):** "We enrolled adults at higher risk of progression because mild COVID-19 in low-risk adults rarely progresses, which would dilute the treatment effect."
2. **Procedure (selection and consent):** "Adults aged 50 years or older with at least one comorbidity (hypertension, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, chronic respiratory disease, immunosuppression) and a positive SARS-CoV-2 PCR within the past 7 days were screened in primary care; written informed consent was obtained."
3. **Safeguard:** "We tracked screen-failure rates by site to monitor for selective recruitment, and a data safety monitoring board reviewed enrolment quarterly."

### 2.3 Interventions

1. **Procedure:** "Participants in the intervention arm were dispensed a budesonide dry-powder inhaler with written and video instructions; the comparator arm received the corresponding usual-care leaflet."
2. **Safeguard:** "Adherence was assessed by self-report on day 7 and day 14, supplemented by inhaler-counter readings at day 14 in a 20% subsample."

### 2.4 Outcomes

1. **Rationale:** "The primary outcome combined urgent care visit, emergency department attendance, or hospitalization for COVID-19, because each component reflects clinically meaningful progression that would change management."
2. **Procedure:** "Outcomes were ascertained by linkage to electronic health records and confirmed by phone call at day 14 and day 28."
3. **Safeguard:** "All primary-outcome events were adjudicated by a blinded committee using prespecified criteria; inter-rater agreement was prespecified at kappa greater than 0.80."

### 2.5 Statistical analysis

1. **Rationale:** "We used a Bayesian analysis with weakly informative priors because the trial was conducted under platform-trial principles with adaptive enrolment."
2. **Procedure:** "The primary analysis estimated the posterior probability of any treatment benefit on the primary outcome under a logistic regression model adjusted for age, sex, vaccination status, and time since symptom onset; the prespecified statistical analysis plan, including stopping rules, was registered before the first interim analysis."
3. **Safeguard:** "Sensitivity analyses included a frequentist analysis with multiple imputation under missing-at-random and a per-protocol analysis."

## Reusable Writing Pattern

For each Methods subsection, follow this order:

1. `Rationale`: state the unresolved question or known bias that motivates the choice.
2. `Procedure-1`: define structure or instrument (eligibility, drug, scale, dataset).
3. `Procedure-2`: describe forward process in execution order (input → steps → output).
4. `Safeguard`: state how bias is controlled with a measurable property.

## Suggested Paragraph Starters

1. Rationale: `We chose [DESIGN / MEASUREMENT / ANALYSIS] because ...`
2. Procedure: `Given [ELIGIBLE PATIENTS / DATA], we first ... then ... finally ...`
3. Safeguard: `[BLINDING / PRESPECIFICATION / VALIDATION] was used; [METRIC] was achieved.`
