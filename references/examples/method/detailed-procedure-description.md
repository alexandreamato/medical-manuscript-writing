# Detailed Procedure Description — Worked Diagnostic Accuracy Example

This example shows how to describe a diagnostic accuracy procedure in execution order with appropriate detail.

```
\subsection{2.3 Index test and reference standard}

% RATIONALE
The index test was a serum biomarker assay for soluble urokinase plasminogen activator
receptor (suPAR), which has been proposed as a non-specific marker of inflammation and
risk of disease progression. We assessed its diagnostic accuracy for 30-day mortality in
adults presenting to the emergency department with suspected sepsis, because routine
risk stratification at presentation is currently based on lactate alone and may
under-stratify patients with normal lactate.

% PROCEDURE (index test)
A peripheral venous blood sample was obtained at the time of triage. Serum was separated
within 60 minutes and stored at -80 degrees Celsius until batched analysis. suPAR was
measured using the suPARnostic Quick Triage assay (ViroGates A/S, Birkerod, Denmark)
according to the manufacturer's instructions. Each batch included two manufacturer-supplied
quality-control samples; runs with QC outside +/- 15% of nominal were rejected. The
prespecified threshold for a positive result was 6 ng/mL, based on the manufacturer's
recommendation and on prior derivation cohorts.

% PROCEDURE (reference standard)
The reference standard was 30-day all-cause mortality, ascertained by linkage to the
national death registry and confirmed by chart review for any participant lost to
follow-up before day 30.

% SAFEGUARD
Laboratory analysts were blinded to clinical outcome. Outcome ascertainers were blinded
to suPAR results. The time interval between the index test and the reference standard was
fixed (30 days). Indeterminate suPAR results (assay errors, insufficient sample) were
prespecified as missing at random for the primary analysis and as positive in a sensitivity
analysis.
```

## Reusable structure

1. **Rationale:** name the proposed marker / model / instrument and the gap in current practice.
2. **Procedure (index test):** sample collection, processing, instrument with model and manufacturer, quality control, threshold definition.
3. **Procedure (reference standard):** definition, ascertainment source, blinded interpretation, time interval.
4. **Safeguard:** blinding of analysts and ascertainers, prespecified handling of indeterminate results.

## Notes

1. Manufacturer name and assay version are mandatory for reproducibility.
2. Quality-control criteria belong in the procedure paragraph, not the supplement.
3. The handling of indeterminate results is a STARD requirement.
