# Methods Subsection — Worked Three-Element Example (Rationale / Procedure / Safeguard)

`Each Methods subsection makes three things explicit: (1) what was done (procedure), (2) why it was done that way (rationale), (3) why the result is credible (safeguard).`

This file walks through one Methods subsection — Outcome ascertainment in a hypothetical RCT of an anticoagulant — annotated with the three roles.

```
\subsection{2.4 Outcome ascertainment}

% RATIONALE: tie the choice of outcome and ascertainment to the study aim and known
% sources of bias.
The prespecified primary outcome was the composite of stroke or systemic embolism over
24 months. Composite outcomes increase event rates and statistical efficiency but require
that the components are clinically meaningful; we therefore restricted the composite to
events that drive treatment decisions in clinical practice.

% PROCEDURE: describe the operational steps in execution order.
Suspected events were captured at every scheduled visit and through participant-initiated
contact, and were ascertained from clinical notes, imaging (CT, MRI, or transoesophageal
echocardiography as appropriate), and discharge summaries. For each suspected event, the
investigator submitted a standardized event-report form within 14 days, accompanied by
de-identified imaging and source documentation.

% SAFEGUARD: state the bias-control mechanism, with a measurable property where possible.
All suspected events were adjudicated by an independent Clinical Endpoint Committee of
three vascular neurologists who were blinded to treatment assignment, with majority
agreement required for confirmation. Inter-rater agreement was prespecified at kappa
greater than 0.80; the achieved kappa was 0.86 across 412 adjudicated events.
```

## Reusable structure

1. **Rationale paragraph:** Why this outcome, this ascertainment method, this timing?
2. **Procedure paragraph:** Exactly what was done, in execution order, naming the sources, instruments, and timing.
3. **Safeguard paragraph:** Blinding, adjudication, validation, agreement metrics, prespecification.

## Notes

1. The rationale sentence prevents reviewers from assuming the outcome was opportunistic.
2. The procedure paragraph allows reproducibility.
3. The safeguard paragraph names the bias-control mechanism and a measurable property (kappa) where possible.
