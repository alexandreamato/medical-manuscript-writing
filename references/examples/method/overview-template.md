# Methods Overview / Opening Template

`The opening paragraph of Methods names the design, the setting, the prespecified primary outcome, and the registration; then maps the rest of the section.`

## Skeleton

```
% One sentence for design + setting + period
We conducted a [DESIGN] at [N CENTRES / SETTINGS] between [DATE] and [DATE].

% One sentence for population + intervention/exposure + comparator + primary outcome
Adults aged [X] or older with [CONDITION] were [randomly assigned / followed] to receive
[INTERVENTION] or [COMPARATOR]; the prespecified primary outcome was [OUTCOME] at [TIMEPOINT].

% Registration
The trial / review was registered (ClinicalTrials.gov NCT...; PROSPERO CRD...).

% Map the rest of the section
Sections [2.1 to 2.x] describe participants, intervention, outcomes, sample size,
randomization and blinding, and the statistical analysis plan.
```

## Worked example (illustrative; fictional)

```
We conducted a multicenter, double-blind, placebo-controlled randomized trial at
38 hospitals in 6 countries between January 2022 and June 2024. Adults aged 18 years
or older with biopsy-proven IgA nephropathy and persistent proteinuria above
1 g/day despite optimized renin-angiotensin blockade were randomly assigned 1:1
to oral budesonide-MR 16 mg daily or matching placebo for 9 months; the prespecified
primary outcome was the change in 24-hour urinary protein-to-creatinine ratio at
month 12. The trial was registered (ClinicalTrials.gov NCT00000000) and the protocol,
including the statistical analysis plan, was published before the primary analysis.
Sections 2.1 through 2.7 describe participants, intervention and comparator, outcomes,
sample size, randomization and blinding, statistical analysis, and ethics and oversight.
```

## Notes

1. The opening paragraph is the closest the Methods section gets to a "TL;DR".
2. Naming the registration before any other detail signals prespecification, which reviewers like.
3. The map sentence is short and concrete; it tells the reader what to expect.
