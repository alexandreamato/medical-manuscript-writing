# Knowledge Gap Version 2 — Older Mechanistic / Observational Evidence Prefigures the Hypothesis

`Version 2: When an older line of evidence (mechanistic, observational, or pathological) raised the hypothesis but did not test it definitively, use that line as conceptual backing and then show why recent evidence still does not resolve the question.`

## Skeleton

```
% State the mainstream / recent line and its limitation
Recent [trials / cohorts] suggest [HEADLINE FINDING]. However, they ... because ...

% Introduce the older line that prefigured the hypothesis
An older line of [mechanistic / observational] evidence already raised the possibility that
[HYPOTHESIS], but those studies were limited by [LIMITATION].

% Bridge to the present study
Whether [HYPOTHESIS] holds in [POPULATION / SETTING] when tested with [DESIGN] is still unknown.
```

## Worked example (illustrative; fictional)

```
Recent randomized trials have shown that intensive blood pressure control to a systolic
target below 120 mm Hg reduces cardiovascular events compared with standard control,
but these trials largely excluded adults aged 80 years or older and patients with
frailty, leaving the benefit-risk balance in this population uncertain.

An older line of observational evidence in nursing-home residents suggested that
aggressive blood pressure lowering was associated with increased falls and orthostatic
hypotension, but those studies were retrospective, used inconsistent frailty
ascertainment, and could not separate the effect of low blood pressure from the effect
of antihypertensive medication burden.

Whether intensive blood pressure control reduces cardiovascular events without
increasing falls in adults aged 80 years or older with measurable frailty has not been
tested in a prospective randomized trial.
```

## Notes

1. Use this version when there is a credible mechanistic or observational backing for your hypothesis.
2. Acknowledge the limitation of the older evidence; do not over-rely on it.
3. The closing sentence specifies population, intervention, outcome, and design — the present study is implicit but unstated until the next paragraph.
