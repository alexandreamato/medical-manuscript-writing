# Introduction Version 1: Define the Entity, Then the Burden

`Version 1: Use when the condition, exposure, or population is unfamiliar to a general medical audience.`

## Skeleton

```
% Define the condition (what it is, who is affected)
[Condition] is [pathophysiological / operational definition].

% State the burden (incidence, prevalence, mortality, disability, cost)
[Condition] affects approximately [N] per 100,000 person-years and is associated with [burden].

% State the current management context
Current management relies on [standard of care], which [achieves / fails at] [clinical target].
```

## Worked example (illustrative; fictional numbers)

```
Idiopathic intracranial hypertension is a syndrome of raised intracranial pressure without
an identifiable structural cause, occurring predominantly in women of childbearing age with obesity.
The annual incidence is approximately 4 per 100,000 women aged 15 to 44 years and is rising in parallel
with the prevalence of obesity, with permanent visual loss in 5 to 10% of patients.
Current management combines weight loss and acetazolamide, but adherence is limited and
a substantial proportion of patients progress despite optimal medical therapy.
```

## Notes

1. The first sentence is operational, not encyclopaedic. Do not bury the definition inside burden statistics.
2. The burden sentence anchors the reader to a population and a magnitude.
3. The current-management sentence sets up the unmet need and is the natural bridge to the gap paragraph.
