# Not Recommended: Abstract Conceptual Framing Without Naming the Design

`Not recommended: Closing the Introduction with only an abstract conceptual frame, without naming the study design, the population, the primary outcome, or the hypothesis.`

## Why this fails reviewers

1. Reviewers cannot tell whether the study is confirmatory or exploratory.
2. Trial-level details (registration, blinding, follow-up) are hidden until Methods, which delays trust.
3. The Discussion ends up making claims that the design cannot support.

## Pattern to avoid

```
% Insight only, no design
Our work reframes how we think about [problem]. By integrating [framework A]
with [framework B], we propose a new paradigm for understanding [outcome].
We show that this approach yields [vague benefit].
```

## Better alternative

```
% Insight followed by an explicit design statement
We hypothesized that [HYPOTHESIS]. To test it, we conducted a [DESIGN] in
[POPULATION] with [PRIMARY OUTCOME] at [TIMEPOINT]. The trial was registered
prospectively (ClinicalTrials.gov NCT00000000) and analysed under the
intention-to-treat principle.
```

## Notes

1. Reviewers reward concrete framing — design, population, primary outcome.
2. Abstract conceptual framings can appear in the Discussion or in conceptual papers, but not as the closing of an Introduction in an empirical study.
3. If a section reads as a press-release-style frame, rewrite it.
