# Knowledge Gap Version 1 — Established Question With Multiple Prior Studies

`Version 1: For an established question with prior trials and cohorts, build the gap as a chain: foundational evidence -> recent evidence -> remaining unresolved question.`

## Skeleton

```
% Establish what is known (the consensus or guideline-level statement)
It is established that [consensus statement; cite seminal trial or guideline].

% Introduce the most relevant prior study and its limitation
However, the [LIMITATION] of these studies leaves [SPECIFIC QUESTION] unresolved
because [METHODOLOGICAL REASON: small sample, short follow-up, surrogate outcome,
non-representative population, residual confounding].

% Introduce a more recent or contradictory study and its limitation
More recent [trials / cohorts] have suggested [FINDING], but [LIMITATION-B] limits
their applicability to [POPULATION / SETTING].

% Make explicit that this remaining question is what the present study addresses
The question of whether [INTERVENTION/EXPOSURE] [VERB] [OUTCOME] in [POPULATION]
under [DESIGN-CRITICAL CONDITION] therefore remains unanswered.
```

## Worked example (illustrative; fictional)

```
It is established that, in patients hospitalized with acute heart failure, intravenous
loop diuretics reduce congestion and improve symptoms.

However, prior trials of high-dose vs. low-dose furosemide and of bolus vs. continuous
infusion have produced conflicting results on 30-day rehospitalization, in part because
sample sizes were small (typically 200 to 400 participants) and follow-up was short.

More recent registry-based analyses have suggested that early initiation, within 60
minutes of arrival, may be more important than dose, but these analyses are subject to
indication bias because patients with more severe presentations may receive both earlier
and higher-dose therapy.

Whether a protocolized early high-dose strategy reduces 30-day rehospitalization in
adults presenting to the emergency department with acute decompensated heart failure
therefore remains unanswered.
```

## Notes

1. Each sentence cites prior evidence (in a real manuscript, with verifiable citations) and links to a specific limitation.
2. Avoid framing prior studies as strawmen. Acknowledge what they got right.
3. The closing sentence states the present study's question precisely; this anchors the rest of the Introduction.
