# Introduction Version 4: Open With Importance and Immediately Expose the Gap

`Version 4: Use when the field is moving fast and the unresolved question can be stated alongside the burden in the opening paragraph.`

## Skeleton

```
% Importance sentence
[Condition / question] [importance statement].

% Summarize how prior trials / cohorts approached the problem
Previous [trials / cohorts / reviews] have shown [headline finding], but ...

% Expose the unresolved question and the methodological reason
Whether [specific question] remains unanswered because ...
```

## Worked example (illustrative; fictional)

```
Obstructive sleep apnoea affects nearly one billion adults worldwide and is associated with
hypertension, atrial fibrillation, and incident cardiovascular events. Continuous positive
airway pressure (CPAP) reliably improves daytime symptoms and apnoea-hypopnoea indices, but
randomized trials have so far failed to show a reduction in major adverse cardiovascular
events, possibly because of low adherence (mean 3 to 4 hours per night) and short follow-up.
Whether high-adherence CPAP, defined as use of at least 5 hours per night, prevents
cardiovascular events in patients with moderate to severe sleep apnoea remains uncertain.
```

## Notes

1. Stating the unresolved question in paragraph 1 is powerful but only works when the field has a clearly accepted shared baseline.
2. The opening must still respect causal calibration: prior trials "have not shown a reduction" rather than "do not reduce".
3. More commonly, the gap is stated in paragraph 2 or 3; use Version 4 only when the topic supports it.
