# Design Version 1 — One Contribution, Multiple Advantages

`Use when one core design choice carries the paper.`

## Skeleton

```
% State the present study in one sentence (design + population + intervention/exposure + outcome)
In this [study type], we [aim] in [population] using [design].

% Point to the trial profile or study flow figure
The trial profile is shown in [Figure 1].

% State the key methodological strength
Our innovation is [METHODOLOGICAL STRENGTH].

% Briefly state implementation (registration, blinding, follow-up duration)
Specifically, [DETAIL: registry / blinding / outcome ascertainment / analysis plan].

% State 1 to 2 advantages over prior evidence
In contrast to prior [studies], this design allows us to [ADVANTAGE 1].

Another advantage of this design is [ADVANTAGE 2].
```

## Worked example (illustrative; fictional)

```
In this multicenter, double-blind, placebo-controlled randomized trial, we evaluated
whether a 12-month course of low-dose colchicine reduces the risk of recurrent
cardiovascular events in adults with chronic coronary disease and elevated
high-sensitivity C-reactive protein. The trial profile is shown in Figure 1.

Our innovation is recruiting on the basis of an inflammation biomarker rather than
clinical risk score alone, which targets the population most likely to benefit from
anti-inflammatory therapy.

Specifically, the trial was registered before enrolment, used central randomization
with concealed allocation, blinded outcome adjudication by an independent committee,
and prespecified an intention-to-treat analysis with a single primary outcome.

In contrast to prior trials of anti-inflammatory therapy in coronary disease, this
design tests a strategy of biomarker-guided patient selection rather than universal
treatment, which addresses the heterogeneity of treatment effect identified in
post-hoc analyses of CANTOS and LoDoCo2.

Another advantage of this design is the long minimum follow-up of 24 months, which
captures recurrent events that shorter trials may miss.
```

## Notes

1. The first sentence states design + population + intervention + outcome — Methods is forecast in plain language.
2. The methodological strength is named in one sentence.
3. The advantages tie back to specific limitations of prior trials, not to abstract claims of novelty.
