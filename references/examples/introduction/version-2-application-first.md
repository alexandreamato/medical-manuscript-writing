# Introduction Version 2: Lead With Burden (Familiar Conditions)

`Version 2: Use when the condition (e.g., type 2 diabetes, ischaemic stroke, breast cancer) is well known to the audience and a formal definition is unnecessary.`

## Skeleton

```
% Open with burden
[Condition] is a leading cause of [outcome] worldwide and accounts for [statistic].

% Optional: append the unmet target
Despite [current standard of care], [residual outcome] remains [unmet target].
```

## Worked example (illustrative; fictional numbers)

```
Stroke is the second leading cause of death worldwide and the leading cause of acquired
adult disability, accounting for 6.6 million deaths annually. Despite the proven benefit of
intravenous thrombolysis and endovascular thrombectomy, only about one third of eligible
patients reach a treatment-capable centre within the therapeutic window, and 30-day
mortality after ischaemic stroke remains close to 10%.
```

## Notes

1. Skip the formal definition; the audience knows what stroke is.
2. The unmet-need sentence gives the Introduction direction.
3. Cite a recent global burden source for the statistics; do not invent numbers.
