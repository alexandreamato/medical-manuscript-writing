# Design Version 2 — Two Contributions

`Use when the paper makes two prespecified contributions (e.g., efficacy plus a key safety contribution; or pooled effect plus a heterogeneity contribution).`

## Skeleton

```
% State the present study and its overarching aim
In this [study type], we evaluated [intervention/exposure] in [population] for [outcome 1].

% State the key methodological strength
Our innovation is [METHODOLOGICAL STRENGTH].

% Point to the figure
The trial profile is shown in [Figure 1].

% Explain contribution 1 and its advantage
Specifically, [HOW CONTRIBUTION 1 IS IMPLEMENTED]. In contrast to previous trials, this design [ADVANTAGE 1].

% Introduce a remaining unresolved sub-question
However, [REMAINING SUB-QUESTION].

% Explain contribution 2 as the response
To address this, we additionally prespecified [CONTRIBUTION 2: e.g., a co-primary safety outcome,
a key subgroup, an individual-participant data analysis].
```

## Worked example (illustrative; fictional)

```
In this multicenter randomized trial, we evaluated whether early restrictive fluid
resuscitation reduces 90-day mortality in adults presenting to the emergency department
with septic shock.

Our innovation is testing a fluid strategy that integrates dynamic markers of fluid
responsiveness rather than a fixed bolus volume.

The trial profile is shown in Figure 1.

Specifically, in the restrictive arm, additional fluid boluses were given only if
passive leg raising or stroke-volume variation indicated fluid responsiveness; the
standard arm followed the Surviving Sepsis Campaign guidelines. In contrast to
previous trials, this design tests a physiology-guided strategy rather than a fixed
volume threshold.

However, restrictive fluid strategies in septic shock raise concerns about acute
kidney injury, which prior trials were not powered to address.

To address this, we additionally prespecified acute kidney injury within 7 days as a
co-primary safety outcome and powered the trial to detect a clinically meaningful
difference on a non-inferiority margin.
```

## Notes

1. Each contribution gets one sentence with implementation and one sentence with the advantage.
2. The "however" sentence introduces the remaining concern that motivates contribution 2; this is the natural bridge.
3. Co-primary outcomes require multiplicity adjustment; preview the analysis plan in the Introduction's last paragraph or in the Abstract.
